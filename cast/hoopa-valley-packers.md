## Valley Freight Kin

Moving goods through the Klamath-Trinity mountains is hard country work, and nobody handles a pack string with more competence than the families of the Hoopa Valley. These are the haulers, negotiators, and skilled handlers who know every rut, river crossing, and washed-out ridge between the coast and the inland mining camps. Their labor sustains the fragile frontier economy, but they answer first to their kin and their own sovereign obligations.

A packer’s word on the trail is final. They balance the demands of rough travel with the jurisdiction of tribal lands and county borders. When outsiders arrive desperate to move heavy freight or seek passage, they quickly learn that these riders know exactly when a load is worth refusing. 

### Role

Skilled handlers and trail negotiators balancing hard freight against political pressure.

### Traits

- Unmatched understanding of pack animals and steep terrain.
- Deep familial ties that dictate who gets passage and who gets stranded.
- A hard-earned sense for bad weather and worse deals.

### Trail Work

#### Load Refusal
When a merchant demands unreasonable speed or weight, you can leverage their desperation to triple the fee or abandon the contract entirely.

#### The Mule's Ledger
Soothe a panicked or stubborn pack animal to salvage a delicate cargo when the trail turns dangerous.

#### Border Crossing
Navigate the tense jurisdictions between county roads and tribal lands without drawing the immediate ire of local law.

#### Fair Warning
Assess a rough trail and warn riders of impending rockfalls, giving them time to secure their gear.

#### Trade Negotiation
Leverage a stranded camp's need for supplies to negotiate a heavy concession or secure future lodging.

#### Lost Freight Recovery
Track dropped goods or stolen crates across difficult terrain when standard posse trackers lose the scent.

#### Route Denial
Blockade a narrow canyon pass, demanding that strangers explain their business before a single hoof steps forward.

#### Safe Lodging
Call upon kin networks along the Trinity to find a warm hearth and a dry barn, provided you haven't broken your word.

#### The Weight of the Load
Endure brutal weather or exhaustion to ensure that critical, life-saving supplies reach their destination.

#### Official Pressure
When a county sheriff tries to commandeer your string, use your knowledge of local trade reliance to force a standoff.

### Camp Say

> "Let them keep their paper money. When the snow hits the pass, you can't eat a deed."

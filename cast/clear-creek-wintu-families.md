## Clear Creek Kin and Stewards

The families of the Clear Creek basin have weathered Gold Rush fever, forced labor, and relentless settlement pressure, yet they remain anchored to their homeland. They are the seasonal workers, interpreters, and quiet land stewards who understand the soil, the water, and the roads far better than any prospector. Their authority over the land isn't drawn from a deed, but from generations of presence and careful fire stewardship.

They navigate wage labor, trade obligations, and allotment threats while fiercely protecting their kin. In a county that tries to misfile their existence, the Wintu stand their ground, armed with deep land memory and the steadfast right to refuse a hard bargain.

### Role

Landkeepers, seasonal workers, and negotiators balancing kin survival against county pressure.

### Traits

- Deep knowledge of local water, soil, and safe passage.
- Quiet authority that commands respect in negotiations.
- Tight kin obligations that prioritize community protection.

### Trail Work

#### The Right to Refuse
When a foreman or official demands labor or land access, you can leverage community standing to stall their progress without raising violence.

#### Fire Steward
Use your knowledge of the basin to predict weather turns or find old firebreaks when navigating dangerous terrain.

#### Salmon Run Notice
Read the river's signs to secure food or track recent movement upstream that careless prospectors missed.

#### Interpreting the Claim
When examining a county deed or mining warrant, spot the deliberate omissions and leverage them to delay enforcement.

#### Safe Passage
Negotiate a safe route through rugged back-country, provided the riders agree to respect your terms and conditions.

#### Kin Obligations
Call upon nearby family ties to secure temporary shelter or a reliable warning, at the cost of owing a serious favor later.

#### The Warning
Deliver a quiet truth to outsiders about what lies ahead on the trail, giving them a chance to turn back before disaster strikes.

#### Seasonal Memory
Recall old trails and forgotten caches that can mean the difference between starvation and survival during hard winters.

#### Trade Leverage
Offer crucial supplies or local knowledge in exchange for something your community desperately needs from the road.

#### Community Protection
Put your own safety on the line to divert the attention of a hostile posse or angry mob away from your camp.

### Camp Say

> "We were here when the creek ran clear, and we'll be here when their warrants wash away."

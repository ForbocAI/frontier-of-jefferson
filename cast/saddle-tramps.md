## Drift Riders

In the fence shadows and smoky road camps around French Gulch, saddle tramps survive on the precarious edges of the frontier. They are the rootless riders, odd-job hands, and petty thieves who drift between mining camps and livery yards. Carrying loneliness without a shred of noble pretense, they own only what a horse can bear and what the town forgot to ask for. 

These wanderers don’t belong to any one ranch, town, or gang. They trade in rumors, borrowed names, and hard labor for a warm meal. To a posse, they are suspects; to a stranded rider, they are sudden allies. They know the back trails, the price of a dry blanket, and the exact moment when a temporary camp needs to be broken down before the law arrives.

### Role

Rootless wanderers, messengers, and temporary allies surviving on odd jobs and rumor.

### Traits

- Constantly watchful and ready to break camp at a moment's notice.
- Highly skilled at caring for horses and stretching meager rations.
- Fluent in the currency of rumors, borrowed names, and quiet desperation.

### Trail Work

#### Borrowed Name
Introduce yourself with a dead man's name to slip past a county checkpoint or dodge a bounty hunter.

#### Temporary Camp
Find a dry, hidden spot to sleep just out of sight of the main road, keeping the fire low and the horses quiet.

#### Horse Care
Treat a lame mount or push a tired horse just a little further without breaking its spirit.

#### Rumor Trade
Exchange a piece of camp gossip for a plate of beans or a warning about the local sheriff.

#### Soft Crimes
Steal a forgotten saddle blanket, a loose chicken, or an unattended bottle without drawing immediate gunfire.

#### Hunger Pangs
Push through brutal exhaustion and starvation to complete a messenger job when others would quit.

#### Hidden Mercy
Offer a sudden, unexpected kindness to a wounded stranger, expecting absolutely nothing in return.

#### Sudden Loyalty
Stand and fight beside a rider who shared their coffee with you yesterday, surprising everyone involved.

#### Odd Job Hand
Talk your way into a day of mending fences or mucking stalls just long enough to case the ranch.

#### Breaking Camp
Erase all signs of your presence and vanish into the brush seconds before the law rides over the ridge.

### Camp Say

> "A good fire costs wood, but a warm bed costs your freedom. I'll take the smoke."

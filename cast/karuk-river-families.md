## Klamath River Households

Along the steep, forested canyons of the northern Jefferson border country, the Karuk families stand as stewards of the Klamath River. These are the men and women whose lives are bound to the salmon runs, the careful practice of cultural burning, and the deep currents of regional trade. They are river workers, fishers, and negotiators who have watched loggers, miners, and lawmen try to carve up the land with paper and ambition. 

Through the rising pressure of outsiders, they maintain their self-governance and dignity. Their households are anchored by generations of knowledge. Whether managing a river crossing or trading cured salmon with a desperate camp, they operate with a practiced, alert authority, completely unwilling to be hurried by a stranger’s timeline.

### Role

River stewards and tough negotiators holding the line on trade and passage along the Klamath.

### Traits

- Deep expertise in river navigation, seasonal fishing, and land management.
- A fierce commitment to self-governance and kin protection.
- Wary, practiced patience when dealing with outsider demands.

### Trail Work

#### River Crossing
Navigate a treacherous stretch of the Klamath to deliver passengers or goods, but only after your price and terms are met.

#### Salmon Run
Use your knowledge of the seasonal catch to provide sustenance for a starving camp, earning a vital favor in return.

#### Cultural Burning
Read the forest and weather to anticipate or carefully manage a fire line, securing safety for your settlement.

#### Outsider Obligations
Remind a stranger of a past debt or broken promise, forcing them to make amends before they can travel further.

#### Trade Negotiation
Turn a desperate traveler's need for provisions into a hard bargain that benefits the entire household.

#### Route Permission
Deny or grant safe passage through your family's stretch of the canyon, using the river's dangers as your leverage.

#### Family Protection
Stand firm against a hostile posse or land surveyor, using numbers and local knowledge to turn them back without a shot.

#### The Warning
Deliver a quiet truth about the river's current or an incoming storm, letting fools drown if they refuse to listen.

#### Gathering the Baskets
Locate hidden, essential materials and resources in the canyon that outsiders simply walk past.

#### Stewardship
Assert your right to manage the riverbanks, deliberately stalling a logging or mining operation until they negotiate.

### Camp Say

> "The water remembers who took too much, and so do we."
